Mary Schepisi is fascinated with nature in all its forms while influenced by concerns for the environment, the female body and a dream of human rights for all.

Mary has showcased exhibitions in Melbourne, New York City, Los Angeles and London. Her work is held in numerous collections.

Recently creating a marvellous range of homewares, using her painting and needleworks to enrich exclusive textiles. Found on maryschepisidesigns.com

As in life, there is no mandatory path through her landscape, no one route of discovery. Given the difficulty of grasping the vastness and variety of place, both metaphorically and actually, one could forget certain presuppositions and embrace Mary’s visualisation.

Two Bays Vineyard is the inspiration for Mary Schepisi’s new exhibition. Exploring the rich undulating topography of her environment through needlework and painting Mary has discovered an entirely different pictorial dimension.

Fragmented stitched lines delineate the lakes and vineyards of her landscape, a world always in seasonal increase and tranquillity.

Mary has created a vocabulary that is both delicate, textual and unique. Her singular visualisation creates a dialogue between needle and paint with new layers of meaning as the views emerge.

Mary’s process of incessant stitching and reworking has created a rhizomic vocabulary that is both delicate, textual and unique.

Mary will paint design and stitch any words, quotes, ideas etc. that you request.

Each custom needlework is unique. You can have the choice of color/colors and thread color; but Schepisi will hand paint/dye each fabric to her own design or shapes….

Size of fabric will depend on words chosen; roughly 20cm x 18cm sizes will vary.

In addition to Mary Schepisi's own art practice, she is an active supporter of art philanthropy. In 2009 Schepisi conceived, organized and curated an art auction that raised $84,000 for Juvenile Diabetes Research Foundation (JDRF).

In 2014 Mary created the New Directions Art Auction a bi-annual charity event to raise awareness and funds for the Australian Prostate Centre (APC).

Six auctions have raised more than $600,000. Even after contributing back to the participating artists.
